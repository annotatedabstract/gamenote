How it works

Three seconds, no alt-tab.

01

Press your key

No window opens. Focus stays on the game, exactly where you left it.

02

Speak your note

It auto-stops when you stop talking, then transcribes locally in seconds.

03

Keep playing

The note lands in a plain text file, stamped with the time, in a folder you control.

The part nobody else does

Notes that know where they are in your footage.

Every note shares the same clock as your recording. So the offset into a clip is just the note time minus the clip's start time. Open the file in your editor and jump straight to the beat. It holds across hourly splits too, because each split is stamped with its own start.

Why not just use a dictation app?

Because they're built for a different job.

Dictation apps
Type into whatever window is focused. Useless behind a game, and they grab focus the moment you trigger them.
Note apps
Make you alt-tab to a window. That breaks the moment, and the moment was the whole point.
AI auto-summaries
Happen after the session and summarize what the recording shows, not the specific thing you noticed while it was happening.

FAQ

The questions that come up.

Do I need a GPU?
No. It runs on CPU and transcribes a short note in a second or two. An NVIDIA GPU just makes it faster.
Does it work over fullscreen games?
Yes. Borderless windowed is the most reliable for both the hotkey and the overlay, which is the common setup for recording anyway.
Is my voice sent anywhere?
No. Transcription is fully local. Your audio and your notes never leave your machine.
Windows only?
For now, yes. The hotkey and the focus-safe overlay are built for Windows first.
Does it need admin?
To catch your key while a game runs as administrator, yes, the app needs to run at the same level. There's a one-time setup note that walks you through it.
